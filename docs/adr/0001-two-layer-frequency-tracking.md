# Two-layer frequency tracking: simple tracker + unified anomaly detector

v1 needs both an always-on "what's loudest right now" readout and a way to flag feedback/resonance problems, but these have different jobs and shouldn't be conflated into one signal. We decided to split them into two independent layers: (1) a simple **Tracked Frequency** that reports the highest-energy frequency per a user-selectable weighting curve (A/C/Z, default A), with no notion of "good" or "bad"; and (2) an **Anomaly Candidate** detector that flags frequencies as narrowband, harmonically unrelated, and sustained over a rolling frame window — without attempting to classify the cause (feedback vs. room resonance) in v1.

We considered attempting cause classification (distinguishing feedback's unbounded growth from a resonance's excitation-then-decay pattern), but this requires tracking decay behavior relative to source excitation, which borders on transfer-function analysis — explicitly out of scope for v1. Unifying under one Anomaly Candidate concept keeps the detector simple while still being actionable: the tech needs to know "this frequency is ringing," not why. Cause classification is deferred until the core system is proven out.
